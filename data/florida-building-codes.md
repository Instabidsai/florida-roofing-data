# Florida Building Code — Roofing Requirements Summary (7th Edition, 2023)

## Overview
The Florida Building Code (FBC) is the most stringent building code for roofing in the United States, developed in response to the devastating impacts of Hurricane Andrew (1992).

## Key Requirements

### Wind Resistance
- **Standard FBC zones**: 150 mph design wind speed
- **HVHZ (Miami-Dade + coastal Broward)**: 180 mph design wind speed
- All roofing materials must be rated for the applicable wind zone

### Underlayment
- **Non-HVHZ**: Minimum one layer of ASTM D226 Type II felt or approved synthetic
- **HVHZ**: Self-adhering modified bitumen (peel-and-stick) required — provides Secondary Water Resistance (SWR)

### Fastening
- **Shingle**: 6-nail pattern required in wind zones above 130 mph
- **Tile**: Mechanical attachment (screws/clips) required — adhesive alone not permitted
- **Metal**: Concealed clip system with wind-rated clips

### The 25% Rule
If more than 25% of a roof's total area is damaged or requires repair, the entire roof must be brought to current code. This often means a full replacement.

### Re-Roofing Limits
- Maximum of one layer of re-roofing allowed
- If existing roof already has one layer, complete tear-off is required
