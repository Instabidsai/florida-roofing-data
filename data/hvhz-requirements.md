# High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) — Florida Roofing Requirements

## What Is HVHZ?

The High Velocity Hurricane Zone is a designated area in South Florida subject to the most stringent building code requirements in the United States. Established after Hurricane Andrew (1992), HVHZ requirements apply to all construction in Miami-Dade County and coastal Broward County.

## Affected Areas

- **Miami-Dade County**: Entire county is HVHZ
- **Broward County**: Coastal sections (east of I-95 in most areas)
- **Palm Beach County**: Not HVHZ (standard Florida Building Code applies)

## Key Requirements for Roofing

1. **NOA (Notice of Acceptance)**: All roofing materials must have a Miami-Dade NOA approval number
2. **Wind Load Design**: 180 mph wind load standard (vs 150 mph for standard FBC)
3. **Enhanced Fastening**: Specific nail patterns, screw patterns, and adhesive requirements
4. **Testing Protocols**: Materials must pass TAS (Testing Application Standards) tests
5. **Secondary Water Resistance (SWR)**: Required on all roofs — peel-and-stick underlayment
6. **Impact Rating**: Required for coastal properties

## Cost Impact

HVHZ compliance adds 10-15% to the cost of a roof replacement compared to non-HVHZ areas:
- NOA-approved materials cost more
- Additional labor for enhanced fastening patterns
- More expensive underlayment (peel-and-stick vs synthetic felt)
- Permit fees are higher in Miami-Dade
